The new icons were drawn in a similar style, and best guesses are made as to what some of the similar sounding weather conditions meant.

Style Notes

  • Hail (#17) usually appears in summer, thus the cloud is tinted red; whereas sleet (#18) occurs in winter, so the cloud is tinted a cool blue.
  • Isolated and scattered apparently describes the coverage (area affected). Isolate is ~10-20%, while scattered is ~30-50%. These are depicted in the icons as distance, i.e. the further the cloud is, the less likely to be affected.
    E.g.: #37 (isolated => distant cloud), #38 (scattered => middle distance), #4 (near).
  • Tropical storm (#1) vs Hurricane (#2). An hurricane has a calm eye in the storm; while a tropical storm usually has not formed an eye.
  • Partly cloudly vs mostly cloudy. These are depicted by hollow cloud, and fully-filled clouds respectively.
  • Clear vs fair. E.g. Clear night #31 has a fully-filled moon to denote a clear (cloudless) sky; whereas the moon in Fair night #33 is hollow denoting some cloud cover.
  • Foggy #20 vs Haze #21. Definitions differ, but in general there is lower visibility in fog (hence gray-filled cloud) than haze (hollow cloud). Dust #19 and Smoky #22 clouds are in sandy light brown colour and dark gray to invoke the parallels of sand dust and black smoke.
